This project analyzes pizza sales data to understand sales performance, customer ordering behavior, and product popularity.
The goal is to provide business insights and actionable recommendations using SQL for analysis and Power BI for visualization.
The project focuses on answering key business questions such as:
When do customers place the most orders?
Which pizza categories and sizes generate maximum revenue?
Which products are best and worst performers?
How can sales and operations be optimized?

🎯 Business Problem
A pizza business wants to:
Track overall sales performance
Identify peak demand days and months
Understand customer ordering patterns
Identify best-selling and worst-selling pizzas
Improve revenue, inventory planning, and promotional strategies

💡 Key Business Insights
1️⃣ Peak Demand Timing
Orders are highest on weekends, especially Friday and Saturday evenings.
Monthly analysis shows January and July as peak order months.
Impact:
Customer demand is time-dependent and seasonal.
Action:
Increase staffing, inventory, and marketing campaigns during peak days and months.

2️⃣ Category & Size Performance
Classic pizza category contributes the highest share of total sales.
Large-size pizzas generate the most revenue.
Impact:
Certain categories and sizes dominate revenue.
Action:
Promote high-performing categories and upsell large-size pizzas through combos and offers.
3️⃣ Best-Selling Products
The Thai Chicken Pizza generates the highest revenue.
The Classic Deluxe Pizza sells the highest quantity.
Impact:
Top products drive a significant portion of total sales.
Action:
Highlight best sellers in promotions and ensure consistent availability.
4️⃣ Poor-Performing Products
The Brie Carre Pizza contributes the least revenue and quantity.
Impact:
Low-performing items increase menu complexity and inventory cost.
Action:
Re-evaluate pricing, promotion, or consider removing weak products from the menu.
